    I hold a B.S. in Computer Engineering from California State University, Fresno and an M.S. in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance from Western Governors University. During my undergraduate studies I served as President of IEEE-HKN Theta Kappa and led research in in-vehicle network security and AES side channel attacks.


    Professionally, I interned at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory as a Test Automation Engineer and again for Software Engineering with the Mission Control Systems Test, Integration, and Deployment Team, focusing on automation, CI/CD, and system reliability.


    My current work centers on security research, reverse engineering, and low-level systems development. I explore iOS internals, ARM architecture, jailbreak-class exploitation techniques, and automated analysis tooling. I also develop backend systems, scrapers, and self-hosted infrastructure—from Proxmox and Docker deployments to custom Stremio add-ons and homelab services.
